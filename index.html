Phase 3: The Mechanics

Past Regrets & Criticism

Use should have and shouldn't have to talk about things in the past that were a good idea, but didn't happen. It is used for both personal regret and criticizing others.

1. Personal Regret

You made a bad decision, and now you feel bad about it.

Situation: I ate too much cake.

"I shouldn't have eaten so much. Now my stomach hurts."

2. Criticizing Others

Someone else made a mistake, and you are judging their past action.

Situation: The government built a bad road.

"They should have planned it better. It was a waste of money."

Class Discussion: What should they have done?

Case A: The Bitcoin Pizza

In 2010, Laszlo Hanyecz bought two pizzas for 10,000 Bitcoins. Today, that is worth hundreds of millions of dollars.

Phase 4: Comprehension

Famous Blunders (Pair Work)

Work with a partner. Read the cases, answer the comprehension questions, and then research your own famous mistake.

1. The Candy Blunder (1982)

In the movie E.T., filmmakers wanted to use M&M's. Mars Inc. turned down the request. Hershey allowed them to use Reese's Pieces instead. After the movie, Reese's sales went up 65%. It turned out to be the best marketing ever.

2. Lost In Space (1999)

The Mars Orbiter disappeared. When scientists looked into it, they found out a terrible mistake: one team used meters, another used yards. The spacecraft went too close to Mars and burned up.

Comprehension Check

1. Which candy company missed out on the opportunity?

Answer: Mars Inc.

2. Why did the Mars Orbiter burn up?

Answer: Because of a measurement system mistake (meters vs yards).
